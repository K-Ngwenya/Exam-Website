Ethical Dilemmas of Software Engineering

Codes of ethics, in all areas, are supposed to be a rigid set of principles that govern how we behave. However, this is almost never the case. Ethics have become more like guidelines. Especially in the world of software. Software engineering as a field of research and a career is still relatively young. Because of this, it seems like we never know what to do when people start to do dodgy things. So we just kind of stand on the side-lines, asking ‘difficult’ questions and being okay when nothing comes of it.

I believe that these dilemmas should not even be dilemmas. If Boeng engineers implement a quick fix to an issue they created and it kills hundreds of people, there should be massive ramifications for that. When Tesla misinforms people about the capabilities of their vehicles and it gets people killed, there should be massive ramifications for that. Instead, these things are let off with slap on the wrist. We are okay with these things because when they come out, because the companies scramble and panic to rectify the issue. I believe watching them squirm like this gives us enough satisfaction to forget about the issue because we forget that they’re freaking out to save their income stream and not because they know they’re wrong.

Let’s look at the Tesla incidents for a minute. Tesla was not upfront about the actual capabilities of their vehicles. This wouldn’t be the first time this occurred in advertising. The real problem here is that people died as a result. So how do we punish this? Apparently, it is stated in the manuals that the cars require constant supervision. It may be hidden behind a few pages of the manual, but it’s there. Is it unethical not to be upfront about such important details or is the consumer responsible for reading and understanding the entire user manual? Where is the line between consumer responsibility and company ethics?

This is what makes ethical discussions so difficult. Even though there should be a solid black line between right and wrong, there are just so many perspectives and variables that turn a simple line into a labyrinth.

My thoughts when I first heard about Tesla’s shenanigans, I believed that they should be shut down. Never to operate again. I mean being dishonest and killing people? That’s despicable. Then I thought about the thousands and thousands of people employed by Tesla. Is taking down an entire company of (probably) innocent people ethical? Then there’s the fact that Tesla technically did tell people in their user manuals. Can we overrule the law because people died? That’s quite a slippery slope and we’ll soon find ourselves pushing the law aside whenever something bad happens. Now even as someone trying to enforce ethical behaviours, you have to worry about doing it ethically.

Topics like ethics make me cringe. I really don’t like them. They just never seem to have an actual solution. A conversation on ethics ends when everybody involved decided they can’t/won’t participate anymore. Not because we’ve all come to an understanding. It almost feels pointless to discuss.

I understand why discussing it is important though. Things aren’t great right now, but without the people asking these tough questions and having these discussions, things could be a hell of a lot worse.

I don’t even know if the first half of this blog post made any sense. I was (and still am) trying to think of this from a million different perspectives at once and it is frying my brain. This is where I give up.
